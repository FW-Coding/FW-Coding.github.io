Experience

Research roles, technical leadership, and community engagement spanning battery modelling, electrochemical characterisation, and energy system design.

Undergraduate Research Assistant

ESE Group, Imperial College London | 2023–2024

Worked in the Electrochemical Science and Engineering (ESE) group at Imperial, focusing on experimental characterisation and computational modelling of commercial Li-ion cells.

  • Characterised commercial Li-ion cells using electrochemical spectroscopy techniques including EIS
  • Developed a complete parameter estimation pipeline achieving ~50 mV simulation error from raw cycling data
  • Customised particle swarm optimisation algorithm for multi-condition parameter fitting
  • Contributed Novonix cycler column mapping to the open-source PyProBE package
  • Gained extensive experience with DFN models, PyBaMM, and PyProBE workflows

Battery Sub-Team Lead

Imperial Racing Green | 2023–2024

Led the battery sub-team responsible for designing, building, and integrating bespoke battery packs for Imperial's electric race car in Formula Student competitions.

  • Led a team of five students designing and building bespoke battery packs and BMS for an electric race car
  • Modelled track energy requirements to determine optimal capacity and discharge rates
  • Performed cell selection, pack assembly (spot-welding, soldering), and system integration
  • Coordinated testing and validation to ensure safe, high-performance operation
  • Contributed to strong team performance in competition

FUSE Intern

Faraday Institution | Summer 2023

Summer internship with the FUSE (Facilities for Universal battery Science Exploration) programme, focused on extending PyBaMM's capabilities and science communication.

  • Implemented the first PyBaMM example for a complex fluid-dynamics style system using finite-volume methods
  • Developed custom Python scripts to extend PyBaMM's capabilities beyond standard electrochemical models
  • Won a Student Science Communicator award for effectively presenting technical work to non-specialists
  • Received best poster award at PyBaMM/FUSE event for clear and engaging visual communication
