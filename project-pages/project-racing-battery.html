Electric Race Car Battery Packs

Battery Sub-Team Lead | Imperial Racing Green | 2023–2026

Led a team of five students designing and building bespoke battery packs and battery management systems (BMS) for Imperial Racing Green's electric race car competing in Formula Student events. This project combined systems engineering, practical fabrication skills, and rigorous safety validation to deliver a high-performance power system.

Design Process

Battery pack design for race applications requires balancing competing constraints: maximizing energy density and power capability while minimizing weight and ensuring absolute safety compliance with competition regulations.

  • Requirements Analysis: Modelled race track profiles to determine energy consumption, peak power demands, and thermal loads
  • Cell Selection: Evaluated commercial cells based on energy density, power capability, thermal performance, and cost
  • Pack Architecture: Designed series-parallel configuration to meet voltage and capacity requirements
  • BMS Integration: Selected and configured BMS for cell monitoring, balancing, and protection
  • Thermal Management: Incorporated cooling strategies to maintain safe operating temperatures during high-power discharge

Fabrication & Testing

Pack assembly required precision and attention to detail. The team performed spot-welding for cell interconnections, soldered bus bars and balance leads, and integrated the pack into a protective enclosure meeting safety requirements.

Extensive validation testing included capacity verification, internal resistance measurement, thermal imaging under load, and safety testing (short circuit protection, overcharge/overdischarge protection, thermal runaway containment).

Competition Performance

The battery pack successfully powered the vehicle through multiple competition events, demonstrating reliable performance under demanding conditions. The team achieved strong results in technical inspection and endurance events, validating our design and fabrication approach.

Team Leadership

Leading the battery sub-team involved coordinating design work, managing timelines and budgets, liaising with other subsystems (powertrain, chassis, electronics), and mentoring junior team members. This experience strengthened my project management and communication skills alongside technical battery engineering expertise.
