Finite-Volume PyBaMM Implementation

FUSE Internship | Faraday Institution | Summer 2023

Implemented the first example of PyBaMM applied to a complex fluid-dynamics style system using a finite-volume method in Python. This work extended PyBaMM's capabilities beyond standard electrochemical models and demonstrated the versatility of the framework for multiphysics problems.

Project Motivation

PyBaMM (Python Battery Mathematical Modelling) is primarily designed for battery electrochemistry, using finite-element and finite-difference methods to solve coupled partial differential equations. However, its flexible symbolic framework has potential for broader applications in energy systems and transport phenomena.

This project explored whether PyBaMM could be adapted to solve fluid dynamics problems using finite-volume methods—a common discretisation approach for conservation laws in fluid mechanics, heat transfer, and mass transport.

Technical Implementation

Finite-volume methods discretise the domain into control volumes and enforce conservation laws over each volume. This approach naturally handles discontinuities and shocks, making it well-suited for convection- dominated problems.

The implementation required:

  • Custom mesh generation for control volume boundaries
  • Flux calculation schemes for convective and diffusive transport
  • Numerical integration of conservation equations over finite volumes
  • Interfacing with PyBaMM's solver infrastructure

Results & Impact

The finite-volume implementation successfully solved a benchmark fluid dynamics problem, demonstrating convergence and accuracy comparable to established finite-volume codes. This proof-of-concept showed that PyBaMM's architecture can support diverse numerical methods beyond its original scope.

Science Communication

Communicating technical work to diverse audiences—from battery researchers to numerical methods specialists—required translating domain-specific concepts and emphasizing broader implications. For this work, I:

  • Won a Student Science Communicator award for effectively presenting technical work to non-specialists
  • Received best poster prize at a PyBaMM/FUSE event for clear and engaging visual communication

These recognitions reflect the importance of not just doing good technical work, but also making it accessible and impactful to broader communities.
